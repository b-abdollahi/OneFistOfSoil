I am Ayan

I am Iranian. I was born, grew up and studied in Tehran. I came to Austria in October 2015 to continue my studies in art and architecture and joined the master program in Kunst Universitat Linz, Raum und Design Strategy. Due to the refugee crisis in 2015 in Europa, the main title of the year in our department is Not Welcome. Our mission is to find our position in this crisis and express it with our artworks. I am from an origin out of Europe. How can I decide to welcome or not welcome, when I am not the host? This question came up in the first encounter with the subject. Assuming a position between welcome or not welcome was nonsensical for me. When I came to Austria as an art student, I had the desire to take action as an adherent of the welcoming debate. However, the main question that occurred to me once I had settled in Austria was, how this could be at all possible when I am not the host? After a while, I decided to assume the same point of view as the refugees. I am familiar with their pains and have doubtless sympathy. We are grains of the same soil.
I aim to take a position as a member of this group, and find a way to communicate with the host: the Austrian citizens. So I started looking for mutual attachments, common senses, or common memories. I searched for something that can simply connect us. I liked to find a way to share empathy regardless of governmental decision for dealing with the refugee crisis. Llike when Austrians spontaneously said welcome to refugees when they crossed the borders to Austria.
I choose Soil. Soil as a powerful medium that simply reminds us of being on earth, as human kind.
Within this call, I ask you to add one fist of soil to the soil that the refugees have carried from land to land on their shoes and clothes. By mixing them, we will build a piece of a dream land where they can share art, culture and feelings with their host.

At first place, I would thank Mr. Ton Motton to give me the chance to participate to the program and help me with this project. Also wise guidance from Mr. Michaeal Obrist is always helped me to advance my vision.
